About Benthota turtle hatchery

All visitors to Bentota enjoy visiting the Bentota Turtle Hatchery Project. The pristine golden shores that entice you to our island also offer several endangered turtle species, like the Olive Ridley and Leatherback turtles, an alluring nesting ground. The project's objective is to preserve and safeguard the eggs from predators and other threats. To find out more about these beautiful and endangered sea creatures, go to the turtle hatchery in Bentota.

What you can witness

The baby turtles are released on the beach at dusk, and you can watch them make their way back to the ocean. Visitors are urged to exercise caution as young turtles are released back into the water. Loud noises, bright lights, and flash photography are prohibited because they could cause baby turtles to mistake them for the moon over the ocean, which would prevent them from finding their way back home.

Conservatory methods

When the eggs arrive at the hatchery, they are carefully buried in sand until they break free of their shells, at which point they are moved and put into tanks where they will spend the first few days of their lives. This increases their chances of surviving by giving them a chance to get stronger before being returned to the perils of the sea. In this way, several turtle species are preserved.
