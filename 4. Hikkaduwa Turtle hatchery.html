About hikkaduwa and what you can do there.

Hikkaduwa, which is halfway between Colombo and Galle, is well-known as the ideal location for a relaxing beach vacation. Many go to Hikkaduwa Beach to jump in and participate in the exciting water sports because of its appealing seas. Similar to the previous example, there are numerous additional locations in the town where you can learn how Hikkaduwa recovered from the horrific consequences of the 2004 Boxing Day Tsunami. Visit Hikkaduwa to take advantage of the sun, sand, surf, and more!

Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatcheries

The Sea Turtle Hatchery and Rescue Center is located a few kilometers north of the main Hikkaduwa town and aims to preserve some of the world's endangered turtle species. It has developed into a full-fledged hatchery and rescue facility with dedicated personnel to study, look after, and safeguard various turtle species.

Conservation Efforts

The hatchery and rescue facility currently cares for five different species of turtles. These species include the Green Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, Leatherback Turtle, and Olive Ridley Turtle.

Modern water tanks are included in the facilities at the sea turtle hatchery and rescue center, which also cares for adult turtle species that have been pulled from the water after suffering injuries. The place where the turtles lay their eggs, which has distinct small mounds of sand piled neatly between one another, is one of the more odd sights in the center. Each of these mounds is closely observed to determine when the turtle eggs will hatch.
