About the magnificient creature

Marine Turtles (a Cold Blooded Reptile) have inhabited the earth for over 100 million years and have evolved from tortoise-like animals. They have a head, neck, fore and hind flippers, and a tail, and their jaws can crush, tear or bite depending on their diet. Marine turtles are cold-blooded animals, so their body temperature is determined by the environment. They have lungs to breathe air and are fast and agile swimmers with long flippers and flattened shells.

Living entirely in the ocean, with huge migration paths, the males turtles never come ashore once they enter the sea as hatchlings. Only the females do, back to the same beach, for the sole purpose of making their nests and laying dozens of eggs after mating at sea. As per the individual and species of turtle, the number of eggs laid varies. Once the eggs are laid and are covered with sand, the female moves back to the sea, never to return.

Sri Lankan Turtles and conservatory programs

Sea turtles are a major part of the world's aquatic life, with seven species living in the ocean and 243 living on land. Five of the seven species are Green Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, Olive Ridley, Loggerhead and Leatherback. Three species of freshwater turtles, Parker's Black Turtle, Sri Lanka Black Turtle and the Soft or Flapshell turtle, can also be found in Sri Lanka.

Turtle Life cycle Facts
  1. Hatching - emerge from Eggs buried in sand and soil
  2. Maturity - reaches sexual maturity
  3. Migration - travel long distance for feeding and nesting
  4. Courtship - perform rituals like biting, swimming and head bobbing to attract mates
  5. Mating - mates in water and females store sperm for multiple clutches
  6. Incubation - Develop eggs for weeks or months depending on temp
