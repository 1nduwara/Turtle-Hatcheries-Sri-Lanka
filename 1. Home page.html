The Turtle hatcheries of Sri Lanka

About the magnificient creature

Marine Turtles have been around for 100 million years. They have a head, neck, fore and hind flippers, and a tail, and their jaws can crush, tear or bite depending on their diet. Marine turtles are cold-blooded animals, so their body temperature is determined by the environment. They have lungs to breathe air and are fast and agile swimmers with long flippers and flattened shells.


Sri Lankan Turtles and conservatory programs

Sea turtles are a major part of the world's aquatic life, with seven species living in the ocean and 243 living on land. Five of the seven species are (Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, Olive Ridley, Loggerhead and Leatherback). Three species of freshwater turtles, Parker's Black Turtle, Sri Lanka Black Turtle and the Soft or Flapshell turtle, can also be found in SriLanka. Turtle conservatory programs are currently being on progress in Hikkaduwa, Benthota and Kosgoda

Turtle Life cycle Facts
  1. Hatching - emerge from Eggs buried in sand and soil
  2. Maturity - reaches sexual maturity
  3. Migration - travel long distance for feeding and nesting
  4. Courtship - perform rituals like biting, swimming and head bobbing to attract mates
  5. Mating - mates in water and females store sperm for multiple clutches
  6. Incubation - Develop eggs for weeks or months depending on temp
