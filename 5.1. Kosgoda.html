Kosgoda
Turtle hatcheries

About Kosgoda turtle hatchery

Along the nation's southern shore, there are 18 turtle hatcheries. One of the earliest turtle conservation initiatives in Sri Lanka is the Kosgoda turtle hatchery, which was established in 1981.It is well known for producing the five species of turtles found on the nation's beaches. The Kosgoda projects envision doing much more to protect the turtle life along the shore than only serving as a nursery. For locals and visitors from other countries to participate in and learn about the processes of turtle hatching, they conduct a variety of activities and volunteer programs.

Being a volunteer in Kosgoda Hatcheries

Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery offers an engaging pastime for visitors with an interest in nature and learning about turtle habitats. It provides lodging, meals, and a variety of leisure activities for a fee. Visitors can participate in routine tasks like looking for eggs, transporting them to the hatchery, tending to the injured, feeding them, and preserving the habitats.

Conservatory methods

Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery protects sea turtle eggs and takes care of fishing-related turtles. Volunteers patrol the shoreline at night to find turtles about to lay eggs. The eggs are transported to the hatchery and developed for 45-60 days. Baby turtles are released back into the water in late evenings to reduce predation.
